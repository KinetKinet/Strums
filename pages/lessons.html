Chapter 1
Parts of the Guitar
Before you start playing, it's important to know your instrument. Let's explore the anatomy of a guitar!

Headstock

Located at the top of the guitar, it holds the tuning pegs used to tune your strings.

Neck

The long part you wrap your hand around. Contains the fretboard where you press chords.

Body

The large part of the guitar. On acoustic guitars, this is where the sound resonates.

Strings

Standard guitars have 6 strings, numbered 1-6 from thinnest (high E) to thickest (low E).

String Names
From thickest to thinnest: E - A - D - G - B - e
A helpful way to remember: "Eddie Ate Dynamite, Good Bye Eddie"
Key Terms
  • →Frets : The metal strips on the neck. Pressing behind a fret changes the pitch of the string.
  • →Nut : The small piece at the top of the neck that keeps strings in place.
  • →Soundhole : The circular opening on acoustic guitars that projects sound.
  • →Bridge : Anchors the strings to the body at the bottom.
Chapter 2
Strumming Technique
Good strumming technique is the foundation of rhythm guitar. Let's get your right hand moving!

Pick Hold

Hold the pick between your thumb and index finger. Keep a firm but relaxed grip.

Wrist Motion

Strum from your wrist, not your elbow. Small, fluid movements sound better.

Downstroke

Brush the strings downward from low E to high e. This is your basic strum.

Upstroke

Brush upward from high e to low E. Lighter than downstrokes — used for rhythm fills.

Basic Strumming Pattern
Start with: D – D – U – D – U
D = Downstroke, U = Upstroke. Count it as: 1 – 2 – and – 3 – and
Practice Steps
  • 1Practice strumming open strings (no chords) to get the motion right.
  • 2Use a metronome or drum track at 60 BPM to keep steady time.
  • 3Add a simple chord like Em and strum along.
  • 4Gradually increase your speed as you get more comfortable.
Chapter 3
Plucking Technique
Fingerpicking adds a whole new dimension to your playing. Let's learn the basics of plucking individual strings!

Finger Position

Use your thumb (p), index (i), middle (m), and ring (a) fingers for different strings.

Thumb Role

Your thumb handles the bass strings (E, A, D). Keep it relaxed and slightly curved.

Rest Stroke

After plucking, your finger rests on the next string. Great for melody playing.

Free Stroke

After plucking, your finger moves away freely. Used for fingerpicking patterns.

Basic Fingerpicking Pattern
Try: Thumb (E) → Index (G) → Middle (B) → Ring (e) → Middle (B) → Index (G)
This is the classic "Travis picking" pattern used in countless songs.
Practice Steps
  • 1Assign each finger to its string: Thumb=E/A/D, Index=G, Middle=B, Ring=e
  • 2Practice the pattern on open strings slowly without a chord.
  • 3Add a G chord and keep the pattern going.
  • 4Try switching chords (G to Cadd9) while maintaining the pattern.
Chapter 4
4 Chords to Start
These 4 chords unlock hundreds of songs. Master these and you're officially a guitar player!
G
Fingers: 2, 3, 4
Frets: 3, 2, 3
C
Fingers: 1, 2, 3
Frets: 1, 2, 3
Em
Fingers: 2, 3
Frets: 2, 2
D
Fingers: 1, 2, 3
Frets: 2, 3, 2
Tips for Clean Chords
  • 1Press your fingertips just behind the fret wire — not on top of it.
  • 2Keep your thumb behind the neck for better reach.
  • 3Pluck each string individually to check for buzzing or muted strings.
  • 4Practice switching between G and C until it feels smooth.
Song Challenge: Try playing G - Em - C - D in a loop. You just played the chord progression to hundreds of pop songs!
